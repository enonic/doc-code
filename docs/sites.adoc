Use Enonic XP to deliver websites natively, without introducing a 3rd party front-end framework.

== Introduction

As an alternative to using 3rd party front-end frameworks like Next.js, you may use Enonic XP and Enonic Apps natively to deploy Content Schemas, and deliver advanced websites.

== Sites vs Webapps

Websites are essentially webapps where the URLs are controlled by editors, rather than the developers. 


== Usage

Unlike XP webapps, XP sites do not have a default controller, but instead rely on resolving controllers based on the content being requested.


== Multiple apps per site
Sites may be powered by multiple apps. The apps may even collaborate on processing and responding to a single request. Similar to webapps, developers can use filters and mappings to take control over URL patterns when needed.
